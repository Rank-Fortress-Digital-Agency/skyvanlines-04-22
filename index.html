How do you pick the best long-distance mover for your moving needs?



With the cost of living on the rise and many people looking to cut costs wherever possible, long distance moves are becoming more and more popular. If you’re planning a long distance move, there are a few things you can do to make sure it’s as cost-effective as possible.

Get rid of anything you don't need.

One of the best ways to save money on a long distance move is to get rid of anything you don’t need or use. Have a garage sale, donate items to charity, or sell them online. This will not only save you money on your move, but it will also help you declutter your new home.

Another way to save money is to pack your belongings. This may seem like a daunting task, but if you start early and plan, it can be surprisingly easy. Plus, you’ll save money on packing materials and labor costs.


Pack efficiently.


When packing for a long distance move, it’s important to be efficient. Don’t pack things you won’t need immediately and be sure to use smaller boxes for heavy items. This will make your move less stressful and more organized.

Label all of your boxes clearly and make a list of what is in each one. This will help you keep track of your belongings and make unpacking easier. Be sure to pack fragile items carefully and keep them away from heavier items. Pack your essentials last so you can access them easily during the move. This includes things like toiletries, medications, snacks, and changes of clothes. Having these items close by will make the transition to your new home much smoother.

Use recycled materials.

If you’re looking for a way to save money on packing materials, consider using recycled materials. Cardboard boxes can be found for free at many stores, and newspapers or towels make great packing material. You can even use old clothes to cushion your items. Just make sure to clean them first!

Another way to reduce the cost of packing is to reuse materials from previous shipments. This includes both boxes and packing material. If you have any friends or family who has recently moved, see if they have any boxes that they would be willing to give you. The same goes for any packing material they may have leftovers, such as bubble wrap or packing peanuts.


Get quotes from multiple companies.


When you’re ready to hire a moving company, get quotes from several different companies. Be sure to compare services and prices so that you can find the best deal.

You can get quotes from different moving companies by searching online or asking friends for recommendations. Once you’ve found a few companies that you’re interested in, contact them to get more information. Be sure to ask about pricing, services, and any special offers that they may have.

When you compare moving companies, be sure to consider all of the factors involved. Price is important, but also consider the quality of service and the company’s reputation. Once you’ve found the right company for your needs, be sure to book early to get the best price and availability.

Ask for discounts.

Many moving companies offer discounts for seniors, students, military personnel, and more. Be sure to ask about any discounts that may be available to you.

You may also be able to get a discount if you book your move during the off-season or mid-week. Moving companies are typically busiest on weekends and during the summer months, so you may be able to save money by scheduling your move for a less popular time.

If you have large items that need to be moved, such as pianos or hot tubs, you may be able to get a discount by renting a truck and doing the move yourself. Just be sure to factor in the cost of renting the truck when comparing prices.

Do it yourself.

If you’re willing to do some of the work yourself, you can save a lot of money on your long distance move. Consider renting a truck and doing the driving yourself, or hiring a company to do the packing and loading while you take care of the driving. You can also save money by shipping your belongings ahead of time. This is especially useful if you have a lot of large items that would be expensive to move, or if you’re moving during the summer when rates are highest.

There are a few things to keep in mind if you’re planning on doing a do-it-yourself move. First, make sure you’re physically up for the challenge. Moving is hard work, and if you’re not used to lifting heavy objects or spending long hours on your feet, it’s probably not the best idea. Second, factor in the cost of renting a truck and any additional equipment you might need, as well as the cost of gas and hotels along the way. And finally, be sure to give yourself plenty of time to plan and execute the move, as it’s not something you want to rush.

Make sure your insurance is up to date.

Before you move, check to make sure your homeowner’s or renter’s insurance is up to date. This will protect your belongings in case they’re damaged during the move. If you’re moving to a new state, you may need to purchase a new policy.

If you’re renting, your landlord’s insurance will usually only cover the building itself – not your belongings. So it’s important to have your own renter’s insurance policy in place before you move in.

Use storage wisely.

If you need to store some of your belongings before or after the move, be sure to shop around for storage options. Compare prices and services so that you can find the best deal. And be sure to plan so that you can avoid paying for last-minute storage.

If you’re moving long distances, you may want to consider renting a storage unit near your old home and another near your new home. This can make the transition easier and save you money on transportation costs.
